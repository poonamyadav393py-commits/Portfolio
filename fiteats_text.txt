Most food delivery apps prioritize variety and convenience but do not offer quick access to truly healthy, goal-based meals. Fitness-focused users often struggle to filter out unhealthy choices, check nutritional value, or reorder clean meals with ease.  Problem Statement Health-conscious individuals in the age group of 25-40 years old with a busy lifestyle—such as gym-goers, working professionals, and fitness enthusiasts—who want to eat clean but don’t have time to cook or shop. They value high-protein, low- carb, and diet-specific options.  Target Audience About Project -

 My Role & Responsibilities - Duration - FitEats is a mobile food delivery app that helps fitness-focused users quickly discover, customize, and order healthy meals that match their dietary goals—such as keto, high-protein, or low-carb diets. The app also shows macronutrient details, prep time, and calorie info so users can make smart decisions on the go.

 I worked as a budding UI/UX Designer. My responsibilities included Researching, Ideating solutions, Wireframing, Prototyping and Testing.

 Research - To better understand how people choose and order healthy food online, I spoke to a few users in person and asked them to fill out a short offline survey. The conversations gave me deeper insights into their habits, preferences, challenges, and what they really want from a healthy food delivery app. This small but useful research helped me make more informed design decisions throughout the project.
