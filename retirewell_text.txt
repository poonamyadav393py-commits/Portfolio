Compare Scenarios Settings Logout Project Overview Retirewell is a web app designed to simplify retirement planning for young professionals and middle-aged individuals in India. It uses interactive calculators, sliders, and visual charts to help users estimate their future savings and expenses with ease. By offering clear insights and personalized recommendations, the app makes financial planning approachable for people without a finance background. My Role & Responsibilities I worked as a UI/UX Designer. My responsibilities included researching, Ideating solutions, wireframing, prototyping and testing. Problem Statement Most people in India, especially young professionals and middle-aged individuals, find retirement planning confusing and overwhelming. Existing retirement calculators and financial apps are either too technical, filled with complex financial terms, or lack personalized insights. As a result, users struggle to estimate how much they need to save, what plans to choose, and how to secure their financial future in a simple, guided way. Target Audience The target audience for RetireWell includes working professionals in India between the ages of 25 and 50 who want to secure their financial future but often find retirement planning confusing.  Testing Feedback User Research Research Goal - The goal of user research for RetireWell was to understand how working professionals in India perceive retirement planning, what challenges they face when using existing financial apps, and what features would make the process easier and more approachable. Research - For this project, I used a qualitative research approach combining secondary research and user surveys/interviews. I reviewed existing retirement apps (like PensionBox and Tata Moneyfy) to identify gaps and studied articles on financial planning behaviors in India. To gather user insights, I prepared a short survey and a few interview questions targeting working professionals aged 25–55, focusing on their awareness, challenges, and expectations around retirement planning tools. Survey - To understand user needs better, I conducted a small-scale survey with 10 working professionals aged 25–50 and followed up with 5 one-on-one interviews. The survey helped capture broad patterns around financial awareness, saving habits, and challenges with existing tools. The interviews provided deeper insights into users’ frustrations. This mix of quantitative (survey) and qualitative (interview) research gave me a clearer picture of user expectations and pain points. Users either don’t save regularly or don’t save for retirement at all. Most have never used a retirement calculator or app. Users struggle most with knowing “how much to save” and get confused by financial jargon. Half of respondents prefer understanding finances through graphs and charts over text-heavy explanations. Users show high willingness to adopt retirement planning if an app made the process simple and guided. Pain Points 1 Confusing financial jargon
 Users found existing retirement calculators filled with complex terms (ROI, annuity, corpus), making them difficult to understand for finance background. 2 Provide personalized insights
 Many users struggled with the most basic question: “How much should I save for retirement?” Current tools don’t give a clear, step-by-step answer. 3 Encourage early planning
 Existing apps often give generic results without considering the user’s age, lifestyle, expenses, or income, making the advice feel irrelevant. 4 Overwhelming interfaces
 Too much data and numbers on a single screen made users feel overwhelmed, leading them to abandon the process. 5 Low trust factor
 Users felt uncertain about whether the numbers shown were realistic or biased toward promoting certain financial products. 6 Limited guidance for beginners
 First-time planners felt lost because tools assume some prior knowledge, instead of guiding them through small, simple steps.. Competitive Analysis - To better understand the market and identify opportunities, I analyzed three existing platforms - PensionBox, Tata Moneyfy, and Groww. This helped me in identifying gaps - step-by-step guided planning, personalized lifestyle-based inputs, or jargon-free explanations. Key Learnings Working on this project taught me the importance of designing for clarity, trust, and empathy—especially in domains like finance where users often feel overwhelmed. I realized that good design isn’t just about creating attractive interfaces, but about simplifying complexity and giving users the confidence to make informed decisions. This project also helped me refine my process of combining user research, competitor analysis, and iterative design to build solutions that are both practical and user-friendly. Most importantly, I learned how crucial it is to keep the user’s emotions and mindset at the center of every design decision. Thank you for viewing ~ Poonam Yadav
