Project Overview  Research  User Flow The Career Roadmap feature is designed to help users discover, plan, and track their professional journey across various career paths such as UX Design, Data Science, Product Management, and Marketing. The goal was to create an interactive and data-backed experience that guides users through every stage of their career - from beginner to expert - by providing insights on essential skills, tools, learning resources, salary ranges, and job demand. The feature also includes an integrated self-assessment to help users evaluate their current readiness and receive personalized recommendations for their next steps. Overall, the design aims to make career planning more structured, motivating, and user-friendly through a clear layout, progress tracking visuals, and engaging elements like badges and milestones. To understand user needs and design an effective Career Roadmap feature, I adopted a combination of primary and secondary research methods. For primary research, I conducted short user interviews with learners and early professionals to explore how they currently plan their careers, what resources they rely on, and the challenges they face during the process. These conversations provided valuable qualitative insights into user motivations, learning behaviors, and frustrations. For secondary research, I analyzed publicly available data from credible online sources such as LinkedIn Learning, Coursera, Udemy, Glassdoor, Ambition Box and Naukri along with industry reports and blogs. The aim was to identify popular career paths, courses, required skills and tools at each level, and understand trends related to salary ranges, job demand, and growth potential both in India and globally. Through short user interviews with learners and early professionals, I discovered that many struggle to plan their career growth effectively. They often feel unsure about where to start, which skills to prioritize, and how to stay consistent while learning. From secondary research using sources like LinkedIn Learning, Coursera, and Glassdoor, I found that most popular career paths—such as UX Design, Data Science, and Product Management- follow a structured journey from beginner to expert. However, many existing platforms fail to present this journey clearly, leaving users without a sense of direction or measurable progress. These findings inspired the idea of a Career Roadmap feature that combines structured learning paths, progress tracking, and motivation to help users confidently grow in their chosen careers. UX logic & Design Choices The Career Roadmap feature was designed with a clear focus on guiding users through a structured, motivational learning journey. The UX logic emphasizes simplicity, clarity, and engagement. Starting from the Career Dashboard, users can easily explore various paths and select one that matches their interest - for example, UI/UX Design.

 Once inside the chosen career, the roadmap is broken into four progressive stages (Beginner → Expert), allowing learners to visualize their growth path. Each stage highlights duration, key skills, tools, and recommended external courses, giving users both direction and flexibility. The inclusion of “Mark as Completed” and a progress bar supports a sense of accomplishment and progress tracking.

 The interface follows a minimal and clean visual design, using a calm, modern color palette with accent highlights to maintain focus and consistency. Motivational micro-interactions such as badges, encouraging messages (“You’re doing amazing — keep the momentum going!”) and stage completion highlights were added to increase user engagement and retention.

 After completing all stages, users are guided to a final assessment , reinforcing readiness and offering next- step clarity. The “Save for Later” option ensures flexibility — allowing users to revisit postponed courses directly within the same roadmap without breaking their learning continuity.

 The combination of simple UI, visual hierarchy, and motivational elements ensures that learning feels intuitive, achievable, and rewarding throughout the user’s journey.  ~Poonam
